Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with wide, flat sheets of pasta layered with meat sauce, cheese, and other ingredients.
The pasta used for lasagna can either refer to the type of noodle or to the dish itself. Lasagna is usually rectangular or
ribbon-shaped and thicker than tagliatelle. It is a popular dish that has been enjoyed for centuries in Italy and around the world.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef and Italian sausage
  • Onion and garlic
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375Â°F.
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, cook ground beef and Italian sausage over medium heat until browned.
  4. Add diced onion and minced garlic to the skillet and cook until softened.
  5. Stir in pasta sauce or crushed tomatoes and let simmer for about 10 minutes.
  6. In a separate bowl, mix together ricotta cheese, grated mozzarella cheese, and Parmesan cheese.
  7. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  8. Layer cooked lasagna noodles on top of the meat sauce.
  9. Spread a layer of the cheese mixture over the noodles.
  10. Repeat layers of meat sauce, noodles, and cheese mixture until all ingredients are used up.
  11. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  12. Remove foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes or until bubbly and golden brown on top.
